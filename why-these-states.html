Why Texas & California.

Why are Texas and California a meaningful pair to compare — and why these districts within them?

[WRITE YOUR OPENING CLAIM HERE — 2 to 3 sentences. Aim for something like: “Texas and California are the two largest state school systems in the country, both highly diverse, but they embody different answers to the question of who should finance and govern schooling. Comparing them isolates the effect of state structure from the effect of student need.”]

A — Why these two states

To write Explain what makes TX and CA a useful pair analytically. Touch on: (1) both are the largest or near-largest state systems, (2) both are highly diverse, (3) they embody different answers to who finances and governs schooling, (4) comparing them isolates state structure from student need. Aim for 2–3 paragraphs.

B — Why LAUSD and HISD as urban cases

To write Make the case that LAUSD and HISD are representative urban districts. Both are large. Both serve majority-Latino, majority-low-income student populations. Both have significant English-learner populations. Holding student demographics roughly constant lets the state structure show through. 1–2 paragraphs.

C — Why Highland Park appears later

To write Explain Highland Park ISD’s role: it is not representative of Texas, it is a within-Texas contrast. It shows what happens when the same Texas rules meet a very different tax base. Flag this explicitly so a reader doesn’t mistake Highland Park for an average Texas district. 1 paragraph.

D — A note on method

To write Brief method note (3–5 bullets or a short paragraph): • State-level comparison is the main frame
• LAUSD and HISD are representative urban district cases
• Highland Park is a within-Texas contrast, not a representative district
• Data years may vary by source; see the Sources page
• Voting / political patterns are treated as contextual, not causal
