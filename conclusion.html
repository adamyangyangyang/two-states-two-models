Chapter 09 · Assessment & Recommendations

Strengths, weaknesses, and what to change.

What are the strengths and weaknesses of each system — and what should change?

[WRITE YOUR OPENING CLAIM — 2–3 sentences summarizing the assessment. Example: “Each system has real strengths and real weaknesses, and neither is simply ‘better.’ California does more to equalize resources but less to coordinate their use; Texas leaves more room for local responsiveness but converts local inequality into educational inequality. The recommendations that follow work from that diagnosis.”]

California · Strengths CA
To write — 3–4 bullets or short paragraph Stronger state equalization. Broader public obligation for schooling. Buffering of schools from local wealth inequality. Need-weighted funding via LCFF.
California · Weaknesses CA
To write Governance complexity (state/county/district layers). Uneven implementation of LCFF’s need-weighted dollars at the school site level. High total spending does not automatically produce equal outcomes. Accountability for how money is used is thinner than accountability for whether it was distributed.
Texas · Strengths TX
To write Clearer local role and local responsiveness. Strong district variation lets communities shape schooling. Real (if uneven) finance reform through litigation. State accountability system is well-defined.
Texas · Weaknesses TX
To write Local wealth still strongly shapes resources. High-need districts have weaker funding capacity. Local control coexists with structural inequality. Recapture equalizes at the top but does not raise the floor enough.

Recommendations

1 · Texas should strengthen state equalization for high-need districts.

To write Concrete mechanism: raise the per-pupil funding floor in the Foundation School Program; expand the need-based weights for English learners and low-income students; use recapture more aggressively as a bottom-up redistributor rather than only a top-down skim. 1–2 paragraphs, grounded in what your data shows.

2 · California should improve governance clarity and accountability for how money is used.

To write Equalized dollars don’t help if they do not reach the students they were weighted for. Recommendations: better site-level reporting, tighter linkage between LCFF supplemental/concentration grants and the services students actually receive, clearer lines of accountability between district and school. 1–2 paragraphs.

3 · Both states should invest earlier in opportunity gaps rather than waiting for later achievement gaps.

To write Pre-K access, wraparound services, teacher recruitment into high-need schools. 1–2 paragraphs.
