Chapter 10 · Sources & Methods

Where the numbers come from.

What data and literature does this project draw on — and what are its limits?

Method notes

  • The main frame is a state-level comparison between Texas and California.
  • LAUSD and HISD are used as illustrative large urban district cases, not as statistical representatives of their states.
  • Highland Park ISD is used as a within-Texas contrast, not as a typical Texas district.
  • Data years vary by source. Census estimates are 2020–2024 and V2024 vintages; SEDA outcomes cover 2008–09 through 2018–19; district finance figures are 2022–23.
  • Voting and political patterns are treated as contextual background, not as causal claims.
  • Per-student revenue comparisons do not adjust for cost-of-living differences between California and Texas.

Educational Opportunity Project at Stanford University. Exploring Educational Opportunity in Texas. Version 6.0, generated 2026. Derived from SEDA.

Both reports are available at edopportunity.org. SEDA assembles state-administered standardized test results in math and Reading Language Arts for grades 3–8, and expresses scores in grade-level units relative to a national baseline.
