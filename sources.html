Chapter 10 · Sources & Methods

Where the numbers come from.

What data and literature does this project draw on — and what are its limits?

Method notes

To write Short bulleted notes that tell a reader what kind of claim this project is making. Suggested items: • State-level comparison is the primary frame.
• LAUSD and HISD are illustrative urban cases, not statistical representatives of their states.
• Highland Park is a within-Texas contrast, not a typical Texas district.
• Data years may differ by source (note the years for each).
• Any voting or political context is treated as background, not as a causal claim.
• Comparisons of per-student revenue do not adjust for cost-of-living; state spending indices exist if you want to flag this.

Data sources

To list For each data point in the site, list where it came from. Recommended sources: • U.S. Census / ACS — demographics, household income, housing values
• National Center for Education Statistics (NCES) — enrollment, finance, NAEP
• Ed-Data (California) — district-level finance for LAUSD
• Texas Education Agency (TEA) — district finance reports for HISD, Highland Park
• CDE DataQuest (California Department of Education)
• The Nation’s Report Card (NAEP)
